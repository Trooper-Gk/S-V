CODE OF ETHICS

The Code of Ethics (CoE) stands as a guide on how to behave ethically, both within the Foundation and outside. The CoE was devised by the Ethics Committee based on experience and common sense, and was approved by the O5 Council on July 19, 1923. The CoE is expected to be followed by all Foundation personnel regardless of position. Failure to comply is punishable; the punishments are listed below in the Offense System (OFS).

The codex (link below) serves as The Law of The Foundation. The Codex must be followed at all times by all personnel. Anyone caught breaking the Codex will face trial with the Tribunal Department, even including violations of the Code of Ethics.

The Code of Ethics is one of the most important Foundation documents. It outlines the conditions of the Ethics Committee as well as all relevant humanitarian rights and power establishments regarding D-Class Personnel and the safety of Foundation Personnel. Responsibility for amending this document is ultimately reserved only for members of the Ethics Committee, and may be granted through emergency powers publicly from the Board of Directors through the public, to which the document is always granted, or to be published by the Ethics Editor(+)

The Code of Ethics stands higher than any Foundation personnel, including the O5 and The Administrator - [if highly needed]. The Code of Ethics must be enforced, no matter what their rank or site. They should have no abusive council or an unethical site.

The O5 Council is a powerful council, but the Ethics Committee is the group that oversees the O5 Council and ensures it is on the right path. It determines if high-ranking personnel commit unethical actions for punishment.

All directors of a department or leadership/department supervisors [TFS-TFL] must enforce the CoE in their own department. If they fail to do so, they may be removed by Ethics or given a Class-A through D offense. For a director to be demoted for failing to enforce the CoE, it must be reviewed by a Vice Editor, Editor, and/or Overseer.

MTF-Omega-01 is allowed to break the CoE and kill an O5 or any high-ranking personnel if majority vote by the Committee Board of Directors or Vice Chairmen+ issue the order) or detain any foundation personnel when requested by Senior Ethics Members+. They must have a reason for their target to be killed. MTF-Alpha-01 can interfere to save the O5's life if this is required by the Committee. This also includes O5s being removed from the Council if they are killed. IF THE SITE IS GETTING REPORTS UP TO 10+ SITE COMMAND MEMBERS BEING CORRUPT, THE ETHICS COMMITTEE CAN TAKE OVER THE SITE IF THERE IS PROOF THE SITE IS CORRUPTED, AS LONG AS THEIR PROOF IS VERIFIABLE, THE COMMITTEE HAS HEARD BOTH SIDES ON EACH REPORT, AND THE REPORTS ARE ENOUGH FOR EACH SITE COMMAND MEMBER TO WARRANT SUCH ACTION.

Offense System

(Designated "punishments" are subject to change at the consensus of the Committee).

Alpha: Class-A offenses result in a written warning.

Bravo: Class-B offenses result in retraining on basics and a reteaching of the CoE.

Charlie: Class-C offenses result in a demotion.

Delta: Class-D offenses result in suspension from Foundation operations and activities (length is up to the Committee and the person's supervisor; this will essentially remove you from the game to give disciplinary notice of a warning).

Echo: Class-E offenses result in exile either from the department or group. This will demote the person to D-Class for 3 or 5 days, and they must be retrained in a different department if provided.

X-Ray: Class-X offenses will result in interrogation, death, permanent removal from the department, or possibly a permanent ban from the game. [Any action that does not result in a ban will permanently send the person to Class-D unless given a second chance by the authority of the Overseer Council or O5 of the Ethics Committee.]

MTF-Omega-01 cannot kill people for no reason unless given orders from an Ethics Committee Vice Editor+ with authorization.

(If the Ethics Committee does not feel that the designated punishment is adequate for the severity of the code violation, they may discuss a harsher punishment with the personnel's superior or O5.)

Build-up of Offenses

Offenses can build up. This is to prevent personnel from continuously breaking the CoE and getting away with only minor punishment. The buildup is as follows:

  • If personnel receive three Class A offenses, they will be subject to a Class B offense.
  • If personnel receive three Class B offenses, they will be subject to a Class C offense.
  • If personnel receive two Class C offenses, they will be subject to a Class D offense.
  • If personnel receive two Class D offenses, they will be subject to a Class E offense.
  • If personnel receive two Class E offenses, they will be subject to a Class X offense.

Why it exists

[SECTION-2] CLASS-D CELL RIGHTS

B - CoE 1:0 - Cell Zone Rights

D-Class are entitled to their own rights in their chamber, and those rights should never be taken away. As long as they are in their cell, a D-Class may do as he/she pleases if it does not hurt personnel, harm themselves, and if they do not have contraband.

B - CoE 1:1 - Cell Zone Harm

D-Class who are harmed in their cells without the previous consent of higher powers (Administrative, and/or Board of Directorates, and/or Phantoms) may have the right to report the occurrence to an Ethics Committee member for unethical action.

B - CoE 1:2 - Unethical Class-D Detainment

D-Class may not be detained inside their cell zone at all (unless they have been ordered to exit their cell by ISD, IA, MTF-Alpha-01, O5+ or Ethics Committee, The Administrator, or EXPUNGED personnel). Unethical detainment usage contains: detaining for no reason, detaining out of the way, detaining inside cell zone, detaining from a far distance, detaining over red line to shoot.

B - CoE 1:3 - Solitary Rules

D-Class may only be put in solitary confinement for a maximum of 15 minutes. Specific violations carry specific SC durations. Class-D attempting to kill or successfully killing an O5 or A-1 or EXPUNGED would be 30 minutes of SC. (If the Ethics Committee feels that punishment isn't enough, they can give up to 40 minutes.)

B - CoE 1:4 - Class-E Human Rights

The Class-Es have no legal rights on the Site/Area. They cannot leave their cell ever and must be fed once every 2 days. Otherwise, the Class-Es can be shot in the leg by anyone who wishes to enter their cell and not see punishments for doing so; they may give Class-Es infections, which would not be illegal. And they are always eligible for termination if they escape their cell.

B - CoE 1:5 - Ethical Line Termination

Only certain personnel may cross the red line. If a D-Class is pushed across the red line, they cannot be terminated. The pusher is subject to termination. Stepping over the line will result in immediate termination unless authorized by Editor+.
